Elaine Zhelan Chen

PhD Candidate · Johns Hopkins Biomedical Engineering

I'm a 6th year Biomedical Engineering PhD student studying how heart cells mature, a fundamental question with direct implications for cardiac disease modeling, regenerative medicine, and therapeutic development.

My projects involve generating AAV induced mosaic heart models, profiling primary cardiomyocytes with single cell RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, and CUT&RUN, and untangling gene regulatory networks through integrative computational analysis, then validating key regulatory circuits in iPSC-derived cardiomyocyte models.

Through my research, I have developed deep expertise in molecular biology, functional genomics, and multiomic NGS data analysis. I also bring hands-on experience across the full modified mRNA workflow, from sequence design and IVT to expression validation, in both academic and industry contexts.

I'm a curious, cross-functional scientist who thrives at the intersection of experimental and computational biology. I'm actively seeking opportunities in biology discovery where cutting-edge wet lab models and data-driven analysis work together.

Ultimately, I'm eager to work alongside industry scientists to translate discoveries into therapies and help accelerate new drugs to patients.
